ANTHONY JOSHUA'S DRIVER CHARGED OVER NIGERIA CRASH THAT KILLED TWO... AND A PARROT

In a bizarre turn of events, Anthony Joshua's driver, Adeniyi Mobolaji Kayode, has been charged with causing death by dangerous driving after a crash in Nigeria that killed two of the boxer's team members and injured Joshua. But here's the kicker: a parrot was also on the scene, and witnesses claim it was seen flying around the wreckage, squawking "Good show, old chap!"

According to police sources, Kayode, 46, was driving a Lexus SUV that collided with a parked truck on the Lagos-Ibadan expressway. Joshua, 36, was a passenger in the vehicle, along with his personal trainer, Latif Ayodele, and strength coach, Sina Ghami, who sadly lost their lives in the crash.

But what really happened at the scene of the crime? Well, eyewitnesses claim that Kayode was seen arguing with a group of street vendors over a disputed bag of fries just minutes before the accident. "I was walking down the road when I saw Adeniyi yelling at those fry sellers," said one witness. "He was like, 'You're not getting away with my fries, you scoundrels!' and then... well, you know."

Meanwhile, Joshua has been left "heavy-hearted" over the loss of his friends, but is reportedly feeling better after a good night's sleep and a hearty breakfast of beans on toast. "I'm just glad I didn't have to fight any pirates on my way to hospital," he tweeted.

And in other news, the parrot that was spotted at the scene of the crash has been identified as a 3-year-old African Grey named Mr. Whiskers. When asked about his role in the drama, Mr. Whiskers simply squawked "Polly wants a cracker" and flew away.

UPDATE: It's been reported that Kayode is now facing additional charges for "reckless and negligent driving," as well as "driving without due care." Oh, and also for being seen with a woman who was not his wife. The plot thickens!
