BREAKING: Trump Tries to Counter China's Influence in Latin America, But It's Like Trying to Stop a Disco Ball with a Feather

In a desperate attempt to reclaim lost ground, Donald Trump will be hosting the "Shield of the Americas Summit" at his golf club on Saturday. Because, you know, nothing says "security interests" like a bunch of rich guys playing golf while eating cheeseburgers.

The summit aims to enlist and expand US friends in the Western Hemisphere and limit Chinese engagement across the Americas. But let's be real, it's not like Trump has any actual strategy or policies that don't involve tweeting angry things at people. He'll probably just end up getting into a Twitter feud with some Venezuelan dictator over who's got the best mustache.

Trump's team will be led by Kristi Noem, the Secretary of Homeland Security (or as we like to call her, "The Woman Who Got Fired"). She'll be joined by conservative leaders from eight nations – Argentina, Paraguay, El Salvador, Chile, Panama, Honduras, Guyana, and Ecuador. Because what's more representative of Latin America than a bunch of guys in cowboy hats and denim jackets?

Colombia, Mexico, and Brazil will be absent, because they're like, "Uh, no thanks, Donald. We've got our own thing going on." But don't worry, Trump will probably just blame them for not being "team players" or something.

The summit is expected to focus on drug trafficking, migration, counter-terrorism, and curbing Beijing's hemispheric influence. Yeah, because that's exactly what the world needs – more military posturing and fewer actual solutions.

Trump has said he wants America to counter China's influence in Latin America. But let's be real, it's like trying to stop a disco ball with a feather. China's got its own economic motivations, and Beijing is channeling substantial investment into the "lithium triangle" – the mineral-rich belt across Argentina, Bolivia, and Chile that holds 56% of the world's lithium reserves.

And while Trump's team will be trying to rally allies around US national security interests, it's hard to take them seriously when they're led by a guy who still thinks climate change is a hoax. I mean, come on, Donald – get with the times, dude!

In short, this summit is just another example of Trump's "America First" policies – which really just means "Donald Trump First." And if you don't like it, well, that's just too bad for you.
