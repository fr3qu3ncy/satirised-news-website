LABUBU MOVIE COMING SOON! (TO THE LAND OF MAKE-BELIEVE)

In a shocking turn of events, Sony Pictures has announced that they are partnering with Pop Mart to create a film featuring those lovable, annoyingly cute Labubu dolls. Yes, you read that right - the same Labubus that have been infesting your social media feeds and causing you to question your life choices.

The movie will be directed by Paul King, who is probably regretting his decision to leave behind the sanity of directing Paddington for this utter madness. The script will be co-written by Steven Levenson, because why not? He's already worked on Dear Evan Hansen, a musical about a teenage boy with an anxiety disorder, and Tick, Tick... Boom!, a film about a guy named Jonathan Larson who died alone in his apartment. This is clearly the next logical step.

But wait, it gets even better! Kasing Lung, the creator of Labubu, will be serving as executive producer on this project. You know, just like any normal person would do when their toy has taken over the world and they're trying to maintain some semblance of control. I'm sure he's not just using his influence to get a cameo in the film as a wise old forest elf who teaches the kids about the importance of being true to oneself.

The movie will combine live-action and computer-generated animation, because why bother with boring ol' live-action when you can have a bunch of CGI Labubus running around on screen? It's like a fever dream come true. And I'm sure the kids will love it, mainly because they're already obsessed with these little creatures that seem to be sucking the joy out of their childhoods.

When asked about this development, Kevin Spacey was overheard saying, "I'll believe it when I see it. But if it's true, I'll just shrug and say 'oh well'. Because what's the point of getting worked up about anything in this mad world?"
