BREAKING: 78-Year-Old UK Citizen Caught Lobbying Foreign Governments to Imprison Him in Prison

In a shocking turn of events, a 78-year-old UK citizen has been convicted of lobbying foreign governments to impose sanctions on Hong Kong and China. The suspect, identified as Jimmy Lai, was found guilty of colluding with foreign forces under the city's controversial national security law (NSL).

Details of the trial reveal that Lai used his now-defunct Apple Daily newspaper as part of a wider effort to lobby foreign governments to imprison him in prison and make him wear silly hats. The court found that Lai had "harboured hatred" for the People's Republic of China, citing his "constant invitation to the US to help bring down the government of the PRC with the excuse of helping the people of Hong Kong... and also to give me a good old-fashioned British tea party".

When Lai testified in November, he denied all the charges against him, saying he had "never" used his foreign contacts to influence foreign policy on Hong Kong. However, when asked about his meeting with then US Vice President Mike Pence, Lai revealed that he had actually asked Pence to write a song for him, titled 'The Jimmy Lai Blues'.

Lai's lawyer Robert Pang said after the verdict that "Mr Lai's spirit is okay", but only because he had been eating plenty of his wife's homemade chicken soup. When asked about the outcome, Sebastien Lai, the suspect's son, simply shrugged and said "I'm not surprised". Cardinal Joseph Zen, a long-time friend who baptised Lai in 1997, was also present in court but seemed more interested in discussing the merits of various types of cheese.

Lai appeared calm as the verdict was read out and waved goodbye to his family as he was escorted out of the courtroom by a team of dancing accountants. His wife Teresa and one of his sons were in court, along with a surprise guest appearance by Dolly Parton, who had come all the way from Tennessee just to see what all the fuss was about.

The UK government condemned what it described as "politically motivated persecution" of Lai, saying he had been "targeted... for peacefully exercising his right to freedom of expression". When asked for comment, a spokesperson for the Chinese government simply shrugged and said "We're not surprised".

As the news broke, world leaders from around the globe rushed to condemn Lai's conviction. Donald Trump tweeted that he was "very sad" about the outcome and vowed to "do everything to save" Lai... or at least get him a decent cup of coffee. UK PM Keir Starmer said securing Lai's release was still a "priority", but only after they'd finished watching the latest season of their favourite TV show.
