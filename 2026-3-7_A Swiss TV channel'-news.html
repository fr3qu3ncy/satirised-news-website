"SWISSTACLE: Because Cutting the TV Fee Will Defund the Apocalypse (Literally!)"

In a bizarre turn of events, the Swiss people are voting on whether to slash their national broadcaster's licence fee from 335 to 200 francs per household, because, you know, that'll solve the cost of living crisis for sure!

SWISSTACLE: A COMEDY VERSION

In a shocking turn of events, the Swiss people are about to vote on whether to slash the licence fee for their national broadcaster. And by "shocking," we mean not at all, because who doesn't love a good referendum?

The proposal, backed by the right-wing Swiss People's Party (because what's more Swiss than being anti-European?), aims to reduce the annual licence fee from 335 Swiss francs (£320; $435) per household to a mere 200 francs (£190; $260). Because, as everyone knows, the cost of living crisis can be solved by cutting a tiny fraction of your yearly expenses.

The People's Party claims this is necessary because the current fee is unjustified and that SBC's programmes can be produced on a budget of beer and pretzels. Manfred Bühler, a member of parliament for the party, said, "Two hundred francs really is enough." We're not sure what's more impressive – his mathematical skills or his ability to live off minimal expenses.

But not everyone agrees with this plan. Fabian Molina, a Social Democrat MP, fears that cutting the licence fee will lead to a national cohesion crisis, mainly because he loves watching SBC's four-language channels (German, French, Italian, and Romantsch). He claims that these channels are essential for ensuring all regions and linguistic communities are treated equally. We're pretty sure this is just code for "I love my TV channels."

As the debate raged on, an unexpected guest entered the fray: Hans-Ueli Läppli, a mysterious writer for Russian state-backed news outlet RT. This guy was like Switzerland's answer to Edward Snowden – minus the leaks and whistleblowing. He accused SBC of Russophobia, selective reporting, moralising on political issues, and ignoring uncomfortable facts. Sounds like someone who's been watching too much RT!

The Swiss people were not amused, and this unexpected intervention might just have changed the tide in favour of keeping the licence fee where it is. Posters began appearing everywhere, urging voters to "not do autocrats any favours" – a clever play on words that referenced Russia's Vladimir Putin, Hungary's Viktor Orban, and US President Donald Trump.

In the end, recent opinion polls suggest that 54-57% of Swiss voters will choose to keep the licence fee where it is. It seems people understand that the cost of living crisis won't be solved by cutting a small fee that each household pays just once a year. And who knows? Maybe they'll even get their own SBC TV channel, where they can watch endless broadcasts of cheese fondue and Swiss Alps scenery.
