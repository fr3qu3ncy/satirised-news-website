BREAKING: Flash Floods Wreak Havoc in Safi's Old City Centre, But Don't Worry, It's Not All Doom and Gloom!

In a shocking turn of events, at least 70 homes have been inundated with water in the old city centre of Safi, Morocco. But don't worry, folks, it's not all bad news! I mean, who needs dry socks when you can have a good excuse to wear your favorite pair of flip flops?

According to eyewitnesses (who were too busy enjoying the impromptu aquatic party to take pictures), the floodwaters swept through the streets like a scene from The Blob. Cars and mounds of rubbish were seen floating along, much like a flotilla of trash pandas on a mission to save the world.

"It was like the apocalypse," said one resident, who wished to remain anonymous but promised to return with a decent bottle of wine next time they spoke to the press. "I mean, I've lost all my clothes... again. Only my neighbor gave me some to cover myself. I have nothing left... except for this sweet new pair of sandals!"

As officials scrambled to respond to the crisis (which was promptly followed by a series of awkward selfies posted on social media), Moroccan authorities declared a state of emergency... or at least, that's what they called it when someone spilled coffee all over the Prime Minister's notes during an important meeting.

In related news, local residents have taken to calling the flooding "Operation Flip Flop" – because, why not? It's a catchy name, and who doesn't love a good excuse to wear their favorite pair of beachy footwear?

Meanwhile, experts say that climate change is likely to blame for the extreme weather fluctuations (because what's a little rain when you have 37 people to kill?). The North African state has been experiencing seven years of drought in a row, which just goes to show that Morocco knows how to mix it up – with both drought and floods!

This latest downpour is expected to continue on Tuesday, complete with snowfall across the Atlas mountains (because who doesn't love a good mountain-top party?). So, if you're planning on traveling to Morocco anytime soon, be sure to pack your waterproof poncho... or just wear that awesome t-shirt with the picture of Elvis on it. Either way, you'll be ready for anything!
