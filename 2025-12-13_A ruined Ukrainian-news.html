In a shocking turn of events, Russian forces have destroyed the majority of Ukraine's pharmacies by targeting warehouses that supply the country's pharmacies. Yes, you read that right - Russians have gone rogue on pharmacy raids, leaving Ukrainians without their favorite painkillers and beauty products.

According to sources (okay, maybe not "reliable" ones), the Russian army has been secretly training a team of ninja pharmacists who infiltrate warehouses under the cover of darkness. Their mission? To destroy medical supplies worth over $200 million. It's like they're trying to give Ukraine a nasty case of pharmaceutical PTSD.

"We were just doing our daily dose of dispensing medicine when suddenly, BAM! Russian missiles and drones show up out of nowhere," said Dmytro Babenko, acting director-general of pharmaceutical distributor BADM. "It was like they were trying to give us a medicinal migraine."

Optima Pharm's chief financial officer Artem Suprun claims their warehouses have been hit three times this year, with the latest attack destroying over $100 million worth of medical supplies. When asked for comment, Russian officials replied, "Uh, nope, we're just here to help Ukraine... by destroying their pharmacies." Yeah, that makes sense.

As a result of these pharmacy raids, Ukrainians are now forced to rely on black market meds and the occasional raid on grandma's medicine cabinet. It's like they say: when life gives you lemons, make lemonade; but when Russia takes away your pharmacy, just try to find some decent painkillers on the black market.
