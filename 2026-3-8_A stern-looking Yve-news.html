UK Foreign Secretary Yvette Cooper Hits Back at President Trump's Tantrum

In a shocking turn of events, Foreign Secretary Yvette Cooper has finally found her voice and told off the world's most famous toddler-in-chief, Donald Trump. It seems that someone in the UK has decided to take on the role of "Mom" and tell Trump to stop throwing his toys out of the playpen.

Appearing on a Sunday morning chat show with Laura Kuenssberg (who looked like she'd rather be having a root canal without anesthesia), Cooper said, "It won't surprise you that we don't agree with President Trump on every issue... mainly because he's just too busy building his next wall to bother learning about actual politics." Ouch! That hurt!

When asked about the UK's response to the conflict in Iran, Cooper proudly declared, "We're taking decisions based on what's best for Britain – not what's best for a certain orange-haired buffoon who's still trying to figure out how to use Twitter." Burn!

Trump had earlier taken to social media (where he probably thought no one would see it) and tweeted, "The UK is thinking of sending aircraft carriers to the Middle East... We don't need people that join Wars after we've already won!" Yeah, because nothing says "winning" like a good old-fashioned tantrum.

Cooper wasn't having it, though. She shot back, "Well, actually, Mr. Trump, we do think it's a great idea to send some of our fancy war ships to the Middle East – mainly because it'll give us an excuse to use up all those leftover biscuits and tea that are just collecting dust in our naval stores." That was just too rich!

As the news spread like wildfire (or should I say, social media- fire?), people around the world were treated to a delightful display of grown-ups acting like children. In response to the UK's clever jab, Trump took to Twitter again and posted a picture of himself with his arms crossed, looking very displeased – and a giant, red "X" marked across his face, just in case anyone missed the point.

Meanwhile, back in the real world (where actual news happens), Iran launched retaliatory attacks on various locations in the Middle East. Oh dear, it seems like someone's going to have to take away Trump's playpen keys... again!
