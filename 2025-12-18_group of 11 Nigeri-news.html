BREAKING: Nigerian Air Force Personnel Still MIA, But We Found Them at a Binge-Watching Party in a Burkina Faso Water Park

In a shocking turn of events, the 11 Nigerian air force personnel who were detained after their military jet made an unauthorized landing in Burkina Faso have been found... but not exactly where you'd expect. According to sources close to the investigation, the crew was last seen at a secret binge-watching party deep within the Burkinabè water park "Splish-Splash Oasis." Yes, you read that right - a water park.

Apparently, after their detention, the air force personnel were given a special dispensation by the Nigerian government to attend this exclusive soiree in exchange for their promise not to tell anyone about the incident. And boy, did they ever take advantage of it! Insiders claim that the crew had a marathon viewing session of Netflix's "The Office" and devoured an entire pizza buffet.

"We were just trying to relax after our ordeal," said Airman Mohammed, a member of the detained crew. "I mean, who wouldn't want to watch 10 hours straight of Michael Scott's antics? It was therapeutic!"

Meanwhile, Nigerian Foreign Minister Yusuf Tuggar has apologized for the incident and promised that measures will be taken to prevent such unauthorized landings in the future. When asked about the whereabouts of the crew, a spokesperson for the minister simply shrugged and said, "Uh... they're still at the water park. Don't worry, they'll turn up when it's time for their next duty briefing."
