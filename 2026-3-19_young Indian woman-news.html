INDIA'S YOUNG ARE MORE EDUCATED THAN EVER... BUT STILL CLUELESS ABOUT GETTING A JOB (OR HOW TO USE THEIR DEGREES)

Despite India's young people being more educated than ever, they remain clueless on how to get a job because their fancy degrees from WhatsApp-run universities don't quite translate into actual employment.

INDIA'S YOUNG ARE MORE EDUCATED THAN EVER. SO WHY ARE SO MANY JOBLESS? (THE COMEDY EDITION)

In a shocking turn of events, it's been discovered that India's young folks are more educated than ever... and still completely clueless about getting a job. Like, who needs skills or experience when you've got a fancy degree from a university that's 90% run by WhatsApp groups?

According to the latest State of Working India report (which we can only assume was written by a team of super-intelligent, ninja-like economists), there are now 367 million young people between the ages of 15 and 29 wandering around India, looking for work. That's like, a whole lotta unemployed kids... or a really big party that nobody invited you to.

Now, on the bright side, more Indians than ever are enrolled in higher education! Yay, for... um, what exactly? Because it turns out that all those degrees aren't quite translating into actual jobs. Like, who needs job security when you've got a fancy piece of paper and a crippling student loan debt?

But wait, there's more! Women are increasingly entering salaried roles in IT, automobile manufacturing, and business services... or at least, that's what the report says. In reality, most women are probably still stuck making leather wallets for 10 hours a day while trying to keep their husbands happy with homemade biryani.

And don't even get us started on the Indian army's recruitment drives in Kashmir. Like, who needs job stability when you can join the army and risk getting shot by militants for a chance to wear a fancy uniform?

All jokes aside (just kidding, we're not going to stop), India's youth unemployment crisis is no laughing matter. It's time for some serious solutions... or at least, some decent memes about it.

THE STATISTICS:

* 367 million young people in India are either in education or unemployment... and probably playing too much Candy Crush.
* Only a small share of graduates secure stable, salaried jobs within a year. Like, who needs job security when you've got Uber Eats?
* Nearly 40% of graduates aged 15-25 (and 20% of those aged 25-29) are jobless... which is probably why they're all so good at playing Fortnite.
* Women's employment is rising... but mostly in self-employment and unpaid or home-based work. Like, who needs a real job when you can make handmade crafts for the Instagram influencers?

THE CONCLUSION:

India's youth unemployment crisis is like that one aunt at family gatherings - it's just there, being awkwardly silent and not really doing much to help anyone. But hey, at least we've got some good jokes about it!
