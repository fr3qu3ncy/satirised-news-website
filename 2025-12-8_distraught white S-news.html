"I don't buy that narrative that in this area the attacks are against whites only," he said. "If they thought that the black guy had 20,000 rand sitting in his safe, they'd attack him just as quickly as they'd attack the white guy with 20,000 [rand] in the safe."

And what about the black farmers who are actually getting killed by thieves? Well, it seems Trump has declared them "not victims of genocide" and instead blames their lack of security measures for being targeted. "I mean, come on guys," he said. "You should really invest in some better fencing and maybe a guard dog or two. That'll show those thieves who's boss!"

The BBC's Africa Eye division was on the scene to document the chaos, with reporter Claire Mawisa asking Marthinus about his plans for his farm if he moves to the US.
