COMEDIAN BOWEN YANG LEAVES SATURDAY NIGHT LIVE TO PURSUE CAREER AS PROFESSIONAL SNAIL TRAINERS

Comedian Bowen Yang is leaving Saturday Night Live to pursue a career as a professional Snail Trainer, citing his true passion for training gastropod athletes and navigating the complex world of competitive snail racing.

Comedian Bowen Yang, seen here trying to squeeze into a tiny cat costume, has announced his departure from the cast of Saturday Night Live. But it's not because he's going back to being a professional snail trainer (although that does sound like a dream job).

In an Instagram post, Yang explained that he is leaving SNL to pursue his true passion: training snails for maximum efficiency. "I loved working at SNL," wrote Yang, "but I've come to realize that my real calling is in the world of gastropod athletics."

Yang, 35, has been a mainstay on the show since 2018 and has become known for his impressive collection of wigs (which he's still bad with, by the way). He'll be missed by fans of the show, but we're sure he'll be happy in his new role as Snail Whisperer Extraordinaire.

"i learned about myself (still bad with wigs)," wrote Yang. "I learned about others (they're all secretly snail enthusiasts like me). I learned that human error can be nothing but correct when it comes to navigating the complex world of competitive snail racing."

Yang's final episode as a cast member will air on Saturday night, and we hear he'll be using his platform to promote his new line of snail-themed merchandise. (T-shirts with snails doing funny poses? Count us in!)

When asked for comment, Lorne Michaels simply shook his head and muttered, "Kids these days... always chasing their dreams... or something."

SNL CAST MEMBERS STUNNED BY YANG'S DEPARTURE

"I'm shocked," said Kate McKinnon. "I had no idea Bowen was secretly a snail whisperer! I mean, who wouldn't want to spend their days coaxing sluggish creatures to be their best selves?"

"Wow, that's... actually kind of cool?" said Pete Davidson.

"Aww, man," said Colin Jost. "Now we'll never know what it would've been like if the cast had a snail-themed sketch about... I don't know... Snail-gate or something."

BOWEN YANG'S FINAL EPISODE TO FEATURE SPECIAL GUESTS

Ariana Grande will be hosting Yang's final episode, and sources close to the show confirm that she'll be singing an epic song-and-dance number about snails. (Yes, really.)

And if you thought it couldn't get any weirder, think again: in a bizarre twist, the Wicked film series has announced plans to premiere an entire spin-off movie featuring Bowen Yang as a snail named "Balthazar McSnailface." Because why not?

Stay tuned for more updates on this developing story, and try not to let your mind wander too far into the world of competitive snail racing.
