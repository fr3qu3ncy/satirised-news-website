In a shocking turn of events, Portugal's Prime Minister Luís Montenegro was left stunned yesterday as thousands of protesters, led by a dancing elephant named Fernando, took to the streets in Lisbon to voice their opposition to his government's proposed labour reforms.

Fernando, a 3000-pound pachyderm with a flair for disco music, was seen boogieing down the streets of Lisbon while waving a large banner that read "Say No to Labour Reforms!" The elephant's dance moves were so smooth that they managed to distract even the most seasoned protesters from their anger towards Montenegro's plans.

The protests were sparked by the government's proposal to allow employers to roll over temporary contracts for years on end, lift a ban on sacking workers then immediately rehiring them indirectly via outsourcing, and remove a requirement to reinstate employees who were unfairly dismissed. As one protester, Eduardo Ferreira, exclaimed: "This is madness! I mean, have you seen the way Fernando dances? He's got more rhythm than our government!"

Meanwhile, Montenegro was left looking like a fish out of water as he tried to address the protesters. "I thought I had done enough to placate you lot," he said, dripping wet and confused.

The strike was not limited to the public sector, however. Thousands of employees at Portugal's largest factory, VW-owned Autoeuropa, south of Lisbon, voted unanimously last week to back the strike. In a bizarre twist, Fernando the elephant even made an appearance at the factory, where he proceeded to dance on top of a row of parked cars while chanting "Solidarity with the workers!"

As tensions continue to escalate, experts are warning that the situation could get out of hand if not resolved soon. "And now, add to that the presence of a dancing elephant... it's a recipe for disaster!"
