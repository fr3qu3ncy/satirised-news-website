THE DISAPPEARANCE OF NANCY GUTHRIE: A CASE OF "MOM-CRAFT" AND "CINNAMON ROLLS"

In a bizarre turn of events, 84-year-old Nancy Guthrie has been missing for over a month, and the nation is still trying to figure out what happened. It's like trying to solve a puzzle with too many pieces that don't quite fit. You know, like when you're trying to put together a jigsaw puzzle while simultaneously making cinnamon rolls from scratch.

The story begins with Nancy, who was last seen wearing her favorite "I'm not lazy, I'm just on Arizona time" t-shirt. Her daughter Savannah, a TV presenter and all-around amazing person, has been frantically searching for her mom, leaving no stone unturned (except maybe the couch cushions, which are notoriously difficult to search).

The investigation has led to a series of hilarious missteps, including:

* A delivery man being detained for questioning after the FBI released footage of the suspected kidnapper. He was later cleared because he had an alibi: he was at the Taco Bell down the street, eating a Crunchwrap Supreme.
* A California man being arrested on suspicion of sending fake ransom notes. It turned out he just really likes writing letters and doesn't want to be bothered with actual human interactions.
* The discovery of drops of Nancy's blood on her front doorstep, which some experts believe was caused by a rogue raccoon with a taste for blood.

As the search continues, law enforcement is following thousands of leads, including tips from "mom detectives" who post videos on social media about true-crime cases. These detectives are like the CSI: Miami team, but instead of forensics, they're experts in baking and making cute crafts.

THE "GUTS" OF THE CASE

The case has been dubbed a "cold case" by some, but investigators are confident that they'll crack it soon. After all, as Sheriff Chris Nanos said, "We have information that could eventually lead to solving the case... but we can't share it with you because we're trying not to spoil the ending."

The Guthrie family has offered a $1 million reward for information leading to Nancy's safe return, which is roughly equivalent to the cost of a year's supply of cinnamon rolls.

As one expert noted, "It's not that interest is waning; it's just that hope is starting to fade... like my hair after I got a perm in 1987."
