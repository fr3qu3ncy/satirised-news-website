Scientists Discover Underwater Mountain Range That's Just A Big Rock

Scientists have made a groundbreaking discovery of an underwater mountain range that's just a big rock, as well as golden towers of coral and never-before-seen sea creatures (mostly fish), in a completely-not-life-changing series of events.

BREAKING: Scientists Discover Underwater Mountain Ranges, Golden Towers of Coral, and Never-Before-Seen Sea Creatures (But Mostly Just Fish)

In a shocking turn of events, scientists have discovered an underwater mountain range that's basically just a big rock in the middle of nowhere. The team, led by Dr. James Bell, was on a mission to explore the depths of the Caribbean when they stumbled upon this magnificent feat of geology.

"We were like, 'Wow, look at that! It's a mountain!' And then we were like, 'Wait, how did we not see it before?'" said Dr. Bell in an interview. "But seriously, folks, it's just a big rock."

The team also discovered golden towers of coral, which is basically just a fancy way of saying they found some pretty coral reefs. But hey, at least they're shiny and stuff.

And if that wasn't enough, the scientists also found never-before-seen sea creatures... well, not really. They mostly just found fish. Lots and lots of fish. Like, have you ever seen a whole school of fish swimming around in perfect sync? It's like they're performing some sort of aquatic ballet.

But wait, there's more! The team also discovered an underwater mountain called Pickle Bank, which is basically just a big rock with a silly name. And get this - it's not even sure how close to the surface it is! Like, maybe it's 10 feet deep or something. Who knows?

The scientists were also thrilled to discover that the coral reefs they found are totally unaffected by climate change... except for all the other coral reefs that aren't.

"We're like, 'Hey, look at this reef! It's thriving!' And then we're like, 'Oh wait, all the other reefs are dying.'" said Dr. Bell. "It's like, what's going on here? Is it a coral reef miracle or something?"

In related news, the team also found a massive blue hole that's basically just a big sinkhole in the sea floor. Like, who wouldn't want to explore a giant crater in the middle of nowhere?

And finally, the scientists discovered an extremely steep 3,200m high mountain ridge stretching 70km along the sea bed... or something. Honestly, we're not really sure what this one means either.
