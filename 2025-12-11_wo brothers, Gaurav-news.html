Brothers Who Fled to Phuket After Deadly Nightclub Fire Get a Reality Check

In a bizarre turn of events, the two brothers who fled to Phuket after their nightclub in Goa caught fire, killing 25 people, were detained by Thai authorities. Gaurav and Saurabh Luthra, the owners of the ill-fated Birch By Romeo Lane club, thought they could outrun the law, but little did they know that even a monkey with a bad memory wouldn't forget their faces.

As it turns out, these two brothers were not just any ordinary nightclub owners. No, no! They were actually secretly competing in the Thai version of "The Great British Baking Show" and had to flee Phuket to avoid getting eliminated from the competition. Who knew that running a deadly nightclub fire was a good excuse to get out of baking?

When asked about their disappearance, Saurabh Luthra's social media statement read like a script from a bad Bollywood movie: "We undertake to co-operate with the police authorities... and submit ourselves to the jurisdiction of the police authorities... and the judicial system... of the country." Yeah, sure, buddy! Like nobody noticed you trying to sneak out of Goa without your pants.

The brothers' lawyer, who looked suspiciously like a cross between a snake oil salesman and a used car dealer, said they would return to India on Thursday and "submit themselves to the jurisdiction of the police authorities... and the judicial system... of the country." Yeah, right! Like anyone believed him. He probably promised to give them a free pair of flip-flops with every bribe.
