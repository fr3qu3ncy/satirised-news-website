You Won't Believe What Caused This Deadly Bar Fire...

In a bizarre incident dubbed "Sparkler-gate," a deadly Swiss bar fire was caused by sparklers placed on bottles of champagne near the ceiling, leading to an investigation and eventual charges against the negligent bar owner.

BREAKING: Investigation into Deadly Swiss Bar Fire to Consider Prosecutions for "Sparkler-gate"

In a shocking turn of events, officials investigating the deadly fire at a Swiss bar have revealed that the likely cause of the blaze was a series of sparklers placed on bottles of champagne. Yes, you read that right - SPARKLERS! It seems that the bar's owners thought it would be a good idea to add some extra pizzazz to their New Year's celebration by attaching sparklers to champagne bottles and placing them near the ceiling. Because, why not?

The investigation has been launched into what has come to be known as "Sparkler-gate," with officials promising to leave no stone unturned in their pursuit of justice. "We will get to the bottom of this," said Valais Attorney General Beatrice Pilloud. "If those responsible are still alive, there will be a case opened against them. And by 'those responsible,' I mean the guy who thought sparklers were a good idea."

The fire at Le Constellation nightclub in Crans-Montana killed 40 people and injured 119 others. Witnesses described a chaotic scene as partygoers tried to put out the flames with their drinks, only to realize that they had all been replaced with decaf coffee. "It was like something out of a horror movie," said one witness. "People were running around, screaming 'I didn't get my caffeine fix!' while others were just standing there, frozen in terror."

Meanwhile, officials are still trying to identify the victims using a process called "Disaster Victim Identification." Because, you know, it's not like they could have just used Facebook to figure out who was dead. This process involves a team of forensic specialists, doctors, dentists, and investigators gathering data that allows them to name the dead. It's a bit like a game of "Guess Who," but with more paperwork.

The investigation will also explore whether the bar ceiling complied with building regulations, which is just a fancy way of saying they're trying to figure out if the owner was just being lazy or if there were actual safety concerns. Either way, it seems that the owner's idea of a good time involved sparklers and champagne bottles, because why not?!

As for the families of those missing after the fire, they are still anxiously awaiting updates from officials on Friday evening. "We don't know if he's still alive," said Francesca Barosi, aunt of missing Italian national Achille Barosi. "But we do know that our family is going through a living nightmare."

In related news, Italian golfer Tahirys Dos Santos was severely burned in the fire and has been airlifted to Germany for treatment. Because what's a New Year's celebration without a little bit of golfing? And who needs medical insurance when you have a good story to tell?!

Stay tuned for further updates on this developing story, which will likely involve more sparklers, champagne bottles, and decaf coffee.

UPDATE: The investigation into Sparkler-gate has been upgraded to a Class-A scandal, with officials promising to leave no stone unturned in their pursuit of justice. Meanwhile, the owner of Le Constellation nightclub has issued a statement saying that they are "shocked" by the events that transpired and that they will be taking full responsibility for the incident... after they finish celebrating their New Year's Eve party.

FINAL UPDATE: The investigation into Sparkler-gate has resulted in a series of charges against the owner of Le Constellation nightclub, including reckless endangerment, negligence, and being really stupid.
