In a shocking move that left farmers across the nation scratching their heads, US President Donald Trump unveiled a $12bn farm aid package yesterday. Because, you know, helping struggling farmers isn't enough – it's gotta be a full-blown production number with sequined cowboy hats and a choreographed dance routine.

The package, touted as a "bridge" to help farmers recover from the devastating effects of Trump's trade wars, was met with a collective shrug by the agricultural community. I mean, who wouldn't want a bunch of cash to cover the cost of replacing their worn-out tractors, right? It's not like they have more pressing concerns, like climate change or something.

Trump made the announcement at the White House, where he was joined by Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent, Agriculture Secretary Brooke Rollins and a handful of bewildered-looking congressmen. The event was also attended by several farmers, who were seen sipping on Starbucks coffee and nodding along to Trump's speech like they were attending a high school assembly.

As part of the package, $11bn will be dedicated to one-time payments for row crops through the Farmer Bridge Assistance programme. Because, you see, those row crops are just waiting to be helped by a good ol' fashioned government handout. The remaining $1bn will be set aside to "study" specialty crops – because who doesn't love a good research project?

But wait, there's more! Trump also threatened to slap an additional 5% tariff on Mexico for allegedly violating an agreement that gives American farmers access to water. Because, you know, Mexico is just hoarding all the water and Trump needs it for his gold-plated toilet.

The news was met with a mixture of reactions from farmers across the nation. Some were thrilled at the prospect of getting their hands on some free cash, while others were more skeptical. Mark Legan, a soybean farmer in Indiana, told us that the government aid would "help our bottom line" – but only if he can use it to replace his tractors and buy some new seeds.

When asked about the ongoing trade dispute with China, Trump said, "The farmers don't want aid; they want a level playing field." Yeah, because nothing says "level playing field" like a 5% tariff on Mexico's water supply.

Meanwhile, Beijing has committed to buying at least 12 million metric tonnes of US soybeans by the end of 2025 – but only if they can get it through Trump's trade policies. It's like trying to navigate a obstacle course while being chased by a pack of wild boars.
