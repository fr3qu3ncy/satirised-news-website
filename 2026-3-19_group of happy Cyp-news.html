In a shocking move, Cypriot President Nikos Christodoulides has come out and called the UK's two military bases on Cyprus a "colonial consequence" - but we're pretty sure he still loves a good plate of bangers and mash.

Speaking at an EU leaders' summit in Brussels, Christodoulides said: "When the situation is over in the Middle East we are going to have an open and frank discussion with the British government... about the weather. Yes, that's it. The weather."

The two sovereign bases in the south and south-east of Cyprus, Akrotiri and Dhekelia, have been a point of contention between the UK and Cyprus for years - mainly because they're just so darn big and impressive.

"I mean, who wouldn't want to visit a place with a sign that says 'British Military Base'?" said a local resident. "It's like a holiday in itself."

But seriously, folks, the British bases have been a thorn in the side of Cypriot leaders for years, what with them taking up 98 square miles of land and all.

"We have more than 10,000 Cypriot citizens within the British bases," said Christodoulides. "We have responsibility for those people... to make sure they don't get too bored on a Sunday afternoon."

The UK Ministry of Defence responded to the comments by saying: "Our bases on Cyprus play a crucial role in supporting the safety of British citizens and our allies in the Mediterranean and Middle East. We're also pretty sure we left the oven on at home, so if anyone needs us, we'll be there in a jiffy."

In other news, Cyprus's Defence Minister has been spotted sipping a coffee and looking very pleased with himself.

Meanwhile, a group of locals have started a petition to keep the British bases - mainly because they're just so good at throwing parties.

"We love the Brits!" said one resident. "They bring us lots of nice things... like beer and sausages."
