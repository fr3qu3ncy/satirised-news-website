BRIGITTE BARDOT: FROM FRENCH CINEMA TO INTERNATIONAL FAME (AND SOME OTHER THINGS)

Brigitte Bardot, the French model and actress, has died at the age of 91. But before we get all teary-eyed, let's take a look back at her life and remember that she was not just a pretty face (although, oh boy, was she ever!). She was also a global icon who directors competed to work with... and by "competed," I mean they bribed her with champagne and caviar to appear in their films.

But what really made Bardot famous was her penchant for causing chaos wherever she went. It's said that if you were at a party with Brigitte, you could guarantee two things: 1) someone would get hurt (either physically or emotionally), and 2) the fire alarm would go off... not because of any actual fire, but just because everyone was so excited to see her.

In fact, Bardot's life was like a real-life movie – complete with over-the-top drama, romance, and action. She started out as a teenage model in Paris, where she quickly became known for her sultry gaze and ability to make grown men swoon (a skill that would serve her well throughout her career).

But it wasn't all glamour and excitement. Bardot struggled in the spotlight, much like Kanye West at a Taylor Swift concert. She was also convicted of various crimes, including... wait for it... race-hate crimes! Who knew Brigitte Bardot had such a dark side?

And don't even get me started on her views on LGBTQ+ issues. Let's just say she wasn't exactly the most supportive ally (or, you know, human being). But hey, who needs allies when you've got millions of fans and a successful film career, right?

Bardot retired from acting in the 1960s to focus on her true passion: supporting animal charities. Ah, yes, because nothing says "compassionate" like a former sex symbol turned animal lover. It's not like she was just trying to distract everyone from her questionable past or anything.

As we say goodbye to this international icon, let us remember the most important thing about Brigitte Bardot: she was a master of self-promotion and could make anyone believe that a good ol' fashioned food fight was actually a viable career move. (I mean, who needs acting talent when you've got eggs and flour on your side?)

In honor of Bardot's passing, let us take a moment to reflect on her legacy: the destruction of innocent men with a single glance; the countless hours spent perfecting one's sultry gaze in the mirror; and, of course, the art of making a public spectacle out of absolutely anything.

So here's to Brigitte Bardot – may your memory be as fiery and dramatic as your life was. May we all strive to be half as outrageous and three-quarters as successful as you were. And may we never forget the true meaning of "French cinema" (i.e., a film where the protagonist is beautiful, charming, and somehow manages to get into absurdly complex relationships with their co-stars).
