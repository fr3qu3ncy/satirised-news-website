According to Israeli officials, the INGOs were asked to hand over "complete" information about their employees, including their favorite pizza toppings and Netflix passwords. When they failed to comply, Israel was like, "Okay, bye-bye!"

Among those affected are some of the biggest aid agencies in the business, including ActionAid, International Rescue Committee, Médecins Sans Frontières (aka Doctors Without Borders), and Norwegian Refugee Council.

These organizations are now facing a 60-day deadline to shut down their operations in Gaza. And let's be real, folks – without these INGOs, it'll be like trying to find a decent Wi-Fi signal at the Gaza Strip: impossible!

The move has been slammed by 10 countries, including the UK, France, Canada, and Sweden, which have jointly condemned Israel's actions as "unacceptable". The EU's humanitarian chief, Hadja Lahbib, even went so far as to say that blocking these aid groups is like "blocking life-saving aid" – pretty harsh words, if you ask us!

But wait, there's more! UN human rights chief Volker Türk called the INGO suspensions "outrageous" and "arbitrary", saying they made an already intolerable situation even worse for the people of Gaza.

And in a bizarre twist, Israeli officials have accused Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF) of being linked to terrorist organizations – without providing any evidence, of course. MSF has responded by saying it would never knowingly employ people engaging in military activity... and that publicly making such claims without substantiated evidence is just plain naughty!

So there you have it, folks – Israel's ban on aid groups has sparked a diplomatic row, with countries around the world calling out the Israeli government for its "unacceptable" actions.
