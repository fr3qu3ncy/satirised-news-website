YOU WON'T BELIEVE WHAT JUST HAPPENED AT A SWISS NEW YEAR'S EVE MASS!

In the midst of a somber New Year's Eve Mass for victims of a devastating fire in Switzerland, a surreal series of events unfolded including a surprise appearance by Dolly Parton and a choreographed dance routine to "YMCA" by the Village People.

BREAKING: TEARS AND HUGS AT SPECIAL MASS FOR VICTIMS OF BLAZE ON NEW YEAR'S EVE

In a heart-wrenching display of grief and solidarity, hundreds of mourners gathered outside St Christopher's Church in Crans Montana, Switzerland, for a special Mass to honor the victims of the devastating fire at Le Constellation bar on New Year's Eve.

As the snow-dusted crowd joined hands and wept quietly, a surprise guest appeared - none other than Dolly Parton, who had flown in from Tennessee to show her support. "I'm here to sing a few hymns and offer some country-style comfort," she said, adjusting her sparkly cowboy hat. "Y'all just keep on cryin' and huggin', I'll take care of the singing."

The service was interrupted when several attendees fainted and fell to the ground, overcome by emotion. But fear not, for they were quickly revived by a team of highly trained EMTs, who were also performing a choreographed dance routine to "YMCA" by the Village People.

Meanwhile, Laetitia Brodard, whose teenage son Arthur died in the fire, was comforted by friends and a surprise visit from her long-lost cousin, Bubba. "He's just like my Arthur," she sobbed. "Except he's got a mullet now."

As the mourners processed silently to the bar itself, they were greeted by a giant inflatable chicken wearing a party hat, which seemed out of place amidst the somber atmosphere. "It's a tribute to the victims' love of disco," explained the bar owner, Jean-Pierre.

During the Mass, a group of firefighters and rescue workers in uniform burst into applause as they reached the memorial shrine. But not before they performed an impromptu rendition of "The Safety Dance" by Men Without Hats.

Later that day, the local fire commander, David Vocat, held an emergency press conference to address rumors that he had considered leaving his job after the tragedy. "I'm here to tell you that I've actually joined a band and we're playing a gig at the local pub tonight," he announced. "We'll be performing all our greatest hits, including 'Fire" by Jimi Hendrix."

As the investigation into the fire continues, authorities have revealed that they are looking into possible overcrowding, access to emergency exits from the basement (which was allegedly converted into a giant game of Jenga), and whether there were sufficient fire extinguishers.

But amidst all the chaos, a group of men in black scarves formed a semi-circle to remember one man killed at the bar. "We're here for our colleague," said one of them, struggling to speak. "Stefi was his final path. May he now rest in peace... and may we get some decent Wi-Fi up here."

And so, as the people of Crans Montana come together to mourn and support each other, they are reminded that even in the darkest times, there is always hope - and a good chance of spotting Dolly Parton in her sparkly cowboy hat.
