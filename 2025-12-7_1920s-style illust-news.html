But don't worry, they've got a plan – it involves robots, video games, and a healthy dose of absurdity.

In response, the government has decided to introduce a new line of robots that can help care for these forgetful folks. Because what's more fun than being cared for by a robot that looks like it was designed by a kindergartener on a sugar high?

Well, just think about how much more enjoyable it'll be when the robot starts changing diapers and preventing bedsores – because who doesn't love the smell of a freshly changed diaper?

But wait, there's more! Researchers at Waseda University are also working on a robot that can monitor patients' sleep patterns by being placed under their mattresses. Because what better way to get some shut-eye than with a robot watching over you like a hawk? And if that's not enough, they're also developing a robot that can play music for residents in care homes – which is just a fancy way of saying "we're going to make the old folks dance like fools."

Because nothing says "customer service" like being served by someone who can't remember what they ordered in the first place. But hey, at least the staff is enthusiastic – they're using flowers to remember which table ordered what, because that's a totally reliable and not-at-all-flaky system.

And if all else fails, there's always Pokémon Go. That's right, folks, Japan's elderly are now using video games to get some exercise and have fun. Because nothing says "fun" like running around in public while being chased by digital monsters – it's like a real-life game of " Tag, You're It!"
