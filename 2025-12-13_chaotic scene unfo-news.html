CHAOS ERUPTS AT MESSI EVENT IN INDIA

Well, folks, it looks like the adoring fans of Lionel Messi in India finally got a glimpse of their hero, but not exactly under the most pleasant circumstances. Thousands had paid up to 12,000 rupees (£100; $133) to catch a whiff of his greatness, but were left feeling like they'd been robbed by a group of officials and security who just couldn't get out of the way fast enough.

Imagine shelling out that kind of cash for a selfie with Messi, only to have him whisked away after 20 minutes like he was on a mission to save the world from certain doom. The crowd went berserk, folks! Water bottles were thrown onto the pitch (don't worry, they weren't filled with actual water), and stadium seats were ripped up like confetti at a Donald Trump rally.

West Bengal's chief minister, Mamata Banerjee, was "deeply disturbed" by the whole ordeal. Who wouldn't be? I mean, it's not every day you see grown adults acting like they just lost their last rupee in a game of street cricket.

As for Messi himself, well, he seemed to be having the time of his life – right up until he was escorted away from the scene by his entourage. Who can blame him? I mean, have you seen the prices of those selfie tickets? It's like they were selling moonbeams and fairy dust out there!
