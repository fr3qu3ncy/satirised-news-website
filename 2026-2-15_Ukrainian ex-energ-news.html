BREAKING: Former Energy Minister Detained While Trying to Leave Country with a Sack of Potatoes and a Disco Ball

In a shocking turn of events, Ukrainian authorities have detained former energy minister German Galushchenko while attempting to flee the country with a suspiciously large number of potatoes. Sources close to the investigation revealed that Galushchenko was trying to leave Ukraine on a train, but was stopped at the border by detectives from the National Anti-Corruption Bureau (Nabu). When questioned about the unusual cargo in his luggage, Galushchenko allegedly claimed he was taking the potatoes to a "very important energy conference" in Poland.

However, witnesses on the scene reported seeing Galushchenko attempting to sell the potatoes to other passengers on the train, and one brave soul even accused him of using the potatoes as a makeshift disco ball to distract from his own guilt. "I couldn't believe my eyes," said a shaken passenger. "He was just spinning that potato around like it was going out of style."

Galushchenko's detention comes as he faces legal proceedings after being dismissed in a corruption scandal involving the embezzlement of $100m (£75m) from Ukraine's energy sector. The ex-minister has been accused of orchestrating a scheme to siphon off funds, using them to buy lavish gifts for his family members and, allegedly, to fund his own secret passion – competitive line dancing.

Despite the serious nature of the allegations against him, Galushchenko remains defiant, insisting that he is "innocent until proven guilty...and even then, not so guilty." His lawyer has issued a statement claiming that his client's detention is merely an attempt by Nabu to "harass" him due to his alleged "tough stance on corruption."

Meanwhile, President Volodymyr Zelensky has condemned Galushchenko's actions as "utterly unacceptable and a clear breach of Ukraine's energy policy." When asked for comment, Zelensky quipped, "I think we've finally found the root cause of our energy problems – it was just German trying to make a quick buck by selling potatoes on the black market."

Galushchenko is currently being held in a special unit at Nabu headquarters, where he will be interrogated further about his involvement in the scandal and his alleged love of disco balls.
