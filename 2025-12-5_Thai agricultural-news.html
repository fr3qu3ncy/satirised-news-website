DEAD MAN WALKING: Hamas Finds Guy They've Been Looking For, 3 Months Later

In a bizarre turn of events, Hamas and Palestinian Islamic Jihad "found" Suthisak Rintalak's dead body in northern Gaza while searching for a lost iPhone, amidst a game of grenade-holding by Hamas fighters.

BREAKING: Gaza's Most Wanted Dead Guy Found in Northern Gaza

In a shocking turn of events, Hamas and Palestinian Islamic Jihad (PIJ) have finally found Suthisak Rintalak, the 43-year-old Thai agricultural worker who had been missing since October 7th. And by "found," we mean they stumbled upon his body while searching for a lost iPhone in northern Gaza.

According to eyewitnesses, Suthisak's body was discovered in a field of juicy tomatoes, surrounded by a group of Hamas fighters playing a game of "Who Can Hold This Grenade the Longest." When asked about the situation, a PIJ spokesperson said, "We're not sure what happened. One minute Suthisak was working on the farm, and the next he was... well, you know."

Suthisak's mother, On, was informed of his passing while watching an episode of her favorite soap opera in Thailand. "I thought it was just a commercial break," she said. "But then I saw Suthisak's face on the screen, and I knew something was wrong."

The recovery of Suthisak's body marks a major milestone in the Israel-Gaza conflict. With only one more dead hostage's body left to be handed over, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's office released a statement saying, "We're happy that Suthisak can finally rest in peace... or at least as much peace as you can get in Gaza."

Meanwhile, Israeli President Isaac Herzog tweeted, "Suthisak may be gone, but his spirit lives on in our hearts. Or at least until we get bored and move on to the next hostage situation."

And in a bizarre twist, US President Donald Trump, who was visiting Israel at the time, was heard saying, "Folks, I've got great news for you. Suthisak's body has been found! And let me tell you, it's a big league deal. Believe me, no one knows more about big leagues than I do."

The incident has sparked widespread outrage on social media, with many people calling for an immediate ceasefire in the conflict. Others are simply wondering why anyone would be farming in Gaza in the first place.
