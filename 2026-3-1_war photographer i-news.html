WAR PHOTOGRAPHER PAUL CONROY DIES AS TRIBUTES PAID (BUT HE'S STILL GETTING TROUBLE FROM THE IN-LAWS)

War photographer Paul Conroy has died at 61, but despite tributes pouring in, he's still likely giving his family a hard time from beyond the grave.

WAR PHOTOGRAPHER PAUL CONROY DIES AS TRIBUTES PAID (BUT HE'S STILL GETTING TROUBLE FROM THE IN-LAWS)

Paul Conroy, the war photographer who's been getting a lot of attention lately for being in the 2018 movie A Private War with Jamie Doran playing him as a totally not-at-all-handsome guy, has died at the age of 61. But don't worry, he's still going to be giving his family grief from beyond the grave.

Conroy was killed by a heart attack on Saturday in Devon, where he had been living in a house that was only slightly too small for his extensive collection of dad jokes and war stories. His brother Alan told the BBC that Paul was "always standing up for what was right" – mainly because he loved being the center of attention.

But wait, there's more! The news of Conroy's passing has sparked an outpouring of tributes from all over the world, including a heartfelt message from Clive Myrie, who said he was "utterly devastated" by the news. And by utterly devastated, we mean he spilled his tea and had to get it re-brewed.

Meanwhile, Jamie Doran, the actor who played Conroy in A Private War, took to social media to say that he was "honored to have been a part of Paul's story". Which is code for "I'm still trying to get paid for that movie".

Conroy's wife, three sons, and grandchildren are all still alive and well, but they're probably going to have to deal with a lot of unwanted phone calls from people asking to borrow money or borrow his famous war stories. His friends in the photography community are also reeling from the news, mainly because they'll have to start calling themselves "ex-war photographers" instead of just "war photographers".

But despite all this, we're sure Paul Conroy will be remembered for many things: his bravery on the battlefield, his extensive collection of dad jokes, and his ability to make anyone who met him feel slightly uncomfortable.
