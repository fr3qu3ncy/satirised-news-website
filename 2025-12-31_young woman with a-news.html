Underwater Chess Champions Crowned in Bizarre Tournament

In a bizarre turn of events, the Diving Chess World Championships have taken place in Groningen, Netherlands, where players used weighted pieces and magnetic boards. Because, you know, that's exactly what chess was missing - a healthy dose of water and magnets.

Competitors had to complete each move in one breath before resurfacing for air, because who needs oxygen when you're trying to checkmate your opponent? The current world champion, Zyon Kollen from the Netherlands, was crowned the men's champion, but not without some controversy. Sources close to the tournament (okay, it was his mom) claim that Zyon cheated by using a hidden scuba tank to breathe while making his moves.

The women's title was won by 17-year-old Josephine Damen, who was so excited about her victory that she did a cannonball in celebration and nearly drowned in the process. Witnesses reported seeing her flailing around like a fish out of water (pun intended) before being rescued by a team of highly trained sea otters.

But wait, it gets better! The tournament's organizers had to make an emergency call to the local pizza delivery service when they realized that none of the players had brought any snacks. I mean, who brings snacks to an underwater chess tournament? It was like they expected everyone to just, you know, breathe and stuff while playing.

In other news, Queen Camilla was spotted in the stands, cheering on her favorite player with a "Go Zyon! Go!" sign that she made herself using only twigs and seaweed. When asked about her presence at the tournament, she simply said, "I'm just here to support my old mate Zyon, innit?"

And finally, the tournament's most surprising moment came when the referee had to stop play because a group of dolphins started playing a game of underwater poker in the corner of the pool. It was like they were trying to distract everyone from the fact that they didn't actually know how to play chess.

All in all, it was a wild and wacky tournament that will surely go down in history as one of the most bizarre sporting events ever held.
