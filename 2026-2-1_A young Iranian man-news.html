BREAKING: Iranian Protester Erfan Soltani Released on Bail, Reports Say... Sort Of

In a shocking turn of events, 26-year-old Iranian man Erfan Soltani was released on bail yesterday after being arrested in connection with anti-government protests last month. But don't get too excited, folks, because this is where things start to get weird.

According to Norway-based Kurdish human rights group Hengaw (yes, that's a real thing), Soltani was initially scheduled to be executed within days of his arrest. However, Iran's judiciary came out and said, "Hey, nope, he just faces security-related charges carrying prison terms only." Which is like saying, "Oh, you're going to jail for a few years, but also maybe not."

Soltani's lawyer, Amir Mousakhani (who sounds like a great guy, by the way), told AFP that his client was released on bail after a cool 2 billion tomans (that's around $12,600 or £9,200, just for context) was paid. Because, you know, money makes everything better.

But wait, it gets even better! Reports say Soltani received all of his belongings, including his cellphone, which is probably still got a few selfies from the protests on it. We're guessing he's going to be a little more careful with that phone now.

Now, let's talk about the real heroes here – the Iranian authorities who were so confident in their ability to execute Soltani within days of his arrest. I mean, who needs due process when you've got a death sentence waiting? That's just good old-fashioned authoritarianism at its finest.

And finally, we've got a special mention for US President Donald Trump, who earlier threatened to take "very strong action" if executions were carried out. Because, you know, that's exactly what the world needs – more Twitter rants from politicians.
