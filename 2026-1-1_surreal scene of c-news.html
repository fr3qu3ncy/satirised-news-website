BREAKING: Chaos Erupts Across the Country as Fireworks and Explosives Take Over

In a bizarre turn of events, reports are flooding in that fireworks and other explosives have been thrown across the country, leaving authorities scrambling to maintain order. It's like someone handed out party favors to every 12-year-old boy within a 5-mile radius.

According to eyewitnesses, police officers were pelted with fireworks in Amsterdam, prompting them to don matching Guy Fawkes masks and shout "You'll never take me alive!" while waving sparklers. The head of the Dutch Police Union, Nine Kooiman, was seen sporting a particularly impressive array of burns, which she attributed to her extensive collection of firework-themed tattoos.

In a bizarre twist, the 50-meter high tower of the historic Vondelkerk church collapsed, but not before it was repurposed as a giant disco ball. The neo-Gothic basilica's design, courtesy of architect Pierre Cuypers, featured a fetching neon light show that hypnotized all who laid eyes on it.

Meanwhile, in Rotterdam, the city's eye hospital treated 14 patients – including 10 minors – for eye injuries sustained while trying to gaze too long at the aforementioned disco ball. Two patients required surgery after attempting to perform a daring eye transplant using a nearby sparkler as a makeshift scalpel.

A 17-year-old boy from Nijmegen and a 38-year-old man from Aalsmeer met their untimely demise in separate fireworks incidents, which authorities are still struggling to explain. "It's like they were trying to set off their own personal brand of chaos," said one police spokesperson, shaking his head.

Germany, not to be outdone, reported two 18-year-olds died after setting off homemade fireworks – a feat that would make even the most seasoned pyromaniac proud. Authorities there have since confiscated all remaining fireworks and replaced them with an assortment of whoopee cushions and party horns.

As one resident of Amsterdam quipped, "It's like the whole country decided to turn into a giant game of 'Boom Boom" – a reference to the popular video game where players take turns throwing explosives at each other. And honestly, who wouldn't want to live in a world where chaos is just a thrown firework away?

In related news, a record €129m was spent on fireworks this year alone, prompting experts to speculate that someone (or something) must have been planning this whole thing for months.
