BREAKING: Ethiopian Police Crack Down on Social Media Stars for Being Fashionably Fabulous

In a shocking turn of events, the fashion police have gone wild in Ethiopia. Six popular TikTok creators were arrested by the authorities for wearing "indecent attire" at a public event. Because who needs freedom of expression when you can just wear a nice blazer and call it a day?

The alleged culprits include Adonay Berhane, Wongelawit Gebre Endrias (aka Evan), Yohannes Mekonnen (aka Jahnny), Bereket Tsegaye, Mekdim Dereje, and Girum Gezahegn. These influencers were accused of promoting "shallow culture" by wearing outfits that were deemed too sexy for the Ethiopian public.

According to eyewitnesses, Adonay was seen sporting an open-necked shirt, Wongelawit was braless under an oversized blazer jacket (because who needs a bra in Ethiopia?), and Yohannes was rocking a man-bag like it's nobody's business. The fashion crimes were said to have taken place at the Ethiopia Creative Award 2025 ceremony.

The police investigation was sparked by concerns that these social media stars were corrupting the youth of Ethiopia with their provocative fashion sense. "We cannot allow our young people to be influenced by shallow culture," said a spokesperson for the Ethiopian authorities. "We must protect them from the scourge of open-necked shirts and oversized blazers."

As news of the arrests spread, many Ethiopians took to social media to express their outrage and support for the detained influencers. "Free Adonay and Wongelawit!" tweeted @EthiopianFashionista. "Their fashion choices are not a crime against humanity!"

The incident has sparked a heated debate about freedom of expression in Ethiopia. While some argue that the authorities have overstepped their bounds, others believe that the influencers were simply trying to make a statement with their outfits.

Meanwhile, Adonay's mother, Abeba Gebru, was seen sobbing uncontrollably outside her son's cell. "He's my backbone and an example to Ethiopian youth," she wailed. "I never thought he'd be arrested for wearing a nice shirt!"
