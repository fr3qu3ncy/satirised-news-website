BREAKING NEWS: Afghan Evacuees in Limbo, Accuse US of Betrayal (and Also Asking for Chicken Nuggets)

In a shocking turn of events, over 1,100 Afghans who were evacuated by the US are now stuck in limbo at Camp As-Sayliyah (CAS) in Qatar. And they're not just worried about their promised resettlement to the US - they're also freaking out because someone left the chicken nuggets in the communal fridge unattended.

"We've been waiting for 18 months, and it's like, 'Hey, we forgot about you guys!' said Alia, a former Afghan lawyer who worked with the US government. "I mean, I know we were just cannon fodder in their war against Iran, but still."

The situation escalated when Qatar started getting hit by Iranian attacks, and the evacuees realized they're not exactly safe where they are either. "We've been betrayed," Alia exclaimed. "Not by the American people, but by those in government who had promised to take us to safety in America - or at least give us some decent Wi-Fi."

According to eyewitnesses, the evacuees have taken to wandering around the camp corridors, crying and clutching their precious chicken nuggets. It's like a scene from a bad sitcom.

"This group of people are not just random refugees that showed up in Qatar," said Shawn VanDiver, a US military veteran who runs AfghanEvac, a charity that helps resettled Afghans who aided the US effort. "They were brought there by the United States government and told that they would be moving to the United States - or at least getting some free pizza."

The evacuees have appealed to President Trump (yes, that guy) to make an exception for them due to the volatile situation. But so far, no dice.

"We've tried calling the White House," said Latif, a former US military contractor who worked at a base in Afghanistan. "But all they keep saying is, 'Sorry, dude, you're not getting any chicken nuggets.' I mean, what kind of country doesn't care about its own refugees?"

As for the US state department's statement that it's not forcibly returning any evacuees to Afghanistan - well, let's just say that's like saying a puppy loves belly rubs. "We're not sending you back," they said. "You can stay in Qatar... and also return to Afghanistan if you want." Yeah, because that's exactly what Latif wants.

"I worked honestly and faithfully with the Americans for years," he said, his voice shaking. "And now they're just like, 'Hey, good luck with that whole 'not getting killed' thing!'"

UPDATE: The BBC has learned that a group of evacuees have started a petition to get more chicken nuggets in the communal fridge. As of press time, it had 500 signatures and was still growing.
