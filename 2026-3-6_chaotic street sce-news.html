BREAKING: Huge Traffic Jam Forms on Street in Beirut... and It's Not Just Cars

In a bizarre turn of events, traffic jams have formed on main roads heading north and south of Beirut, Lebanon, after an unprecedented warning to leave the Hezbollah stronghold was issued. But it's not just cars that are causing the congestion – it's also the sheer number of people trying to flee.

Eyewitnesses report seeing families stuck in traffic with their pets, including a cat named Mr. Whiskers and a parrot named Polly. One woman, who wished to remain anonymous, was seen crying on her car seat while holding a stuffed rabbit named Fluffy. "I just can't leave my bunnies behind!" she sobbed.

Meanwhile, the traffic jam has also attracted the attention of some unexpected participants – a group of Elvis impersonators on their way to a costume party in Cyprus. "We heard there was a great deal on gas station snacks," said one impersonator, wearing a sparkly jumpsuit and slicked-back hair. "Now we're just trying to get to the party before they run out of 'Burnin' Love' disco balls."

Israel Steps Up Strikes... Against What?

The Israeli military has launched airstrikes against Hezbollah targets in Beirut's southern suburbs, but it seems they may have gotten their coordinates mixed up. Instead of hitting enemy strongholds, they've been targeting a local bakery famous for its falafel sandwiches.

"We were just trying to get some lunch before the evacuation warning," said the baker, who wished to remain anonymous. "Now our entire stock of pita bread is gone – and we still owe the bank money on that loan!"

Hezbollah Leader Vows to Confront Israel... with a Megaphone

In a bizarre press conference, Hezbollah leader Naim Qassem vowed to confront Israel "to the point of the utmost sacrifice" – but only if they're feeling sufficiently energized. "We'll just use our megaphone to yell at them until they surrender," he said, holding up a bright pink megaphone. "It's the most effective way to get someone's attention, don't you think?"

Traffic Jam Becomes Game of "I Spy"

As the traffic jam continues to grow, drivers have taken to playing games in their cars to pass the time. One group of children was heard shouting "I spy with my little eye something that starts with 'C'!" – only to realize they were all staring at a chicken sandwich on the dashboard.

"It's not exactly the most exciting game," said one child, looking bored. "But hey, at least we're not stuck in this traffic jam forever... are we?"

UPDATE: Traffic Jam Now Over 10 Kilometers Long

As of press time, the traffic jam has stretched over 10 kilometers long and is still growing. Authorities have warned drivers to expect delays of up to an hour – or possibly even longer if they get stuck behind a parade of Elvis impersonators.
