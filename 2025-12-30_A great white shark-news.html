SHARK-GATE SCANDAL ROCKS MEDITERRANEAN - 40 GREAT WHITES KILLED IN NORTH AFRICA!

The Mediterranean Sea is rocked by a scandal known as Shark-Gate, in which great white sharks are being overfished and killed, despite international protection laws, because who needs laws when you've got a fishing industry to take care of?

SHARK-GATE SCANDAL ROCKS MEDITERRANEAN

In a shocking revelation, researchers have discovered that great white sharks in the Mediterranean Sea are facing extinction due to overfishing and illegal fishing. But that's not all - it gets even juicier!

According to sources (okay, fine, it was just some random guy on Twitter), at least 40 great white sharks have been killed in North African fish markets alone. That's like, a whole season of Shark Tank, but with more fins and less suits.

Now, you might be thinking, "But wait, aren't great whites protected by international law?" And to that, we say... well, yes! But who needs laws when you've got a good ol' fashioned fishing industry to take care of? It's like the saying goes: "You can't make a omelette without breaking some eggs... and also killing a few sharks."

The researchers behind this groundbreaking study (which we're pretty sure was conducted by a team of marine biologists, a couple of accountants, and a guy named Dave who just really loves sharks) claim that the Mediterranean white shark population is now critically endangered. Who knew? We thought it was just a bunch of old guys in speedos trying to catch some rays.

But don't worry, folks! The researchers are on top of this shark-gate scandal like a plate of anchovies at a seafood buffet. They're working with their North African colleagues to monitor fishing ports and catch any sharks that might be trying to sell their fins online (because, you know, there's always someone willing to make a buck off a poor, defenseless fish).

And if you're wondering what happens to all those dead sharks... well, let's just say they're not exactly making a splash on the local seafood menu. More like a big ol' splat.
