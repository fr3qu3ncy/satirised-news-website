YOU WON'T BELIEVE What Happened When 100 Strangers Got STUCK in a Japanese Shrine Due to SNOW...

Over 100 people were stuck at a Japanese mountain shrine due to heavy snowfall, but instead of a disaster, it turned into an impromptu slumber party with hide-and-seek games, hot chocolate, and freshly baked cookies.

BREAKING: Visitors of the Mitsumine Shrine Spent the Night There, and it was a Real Party

In a shocking turn of events, more than 100 people ended up sheltering in a shrine on a mountain top in Japan after roads around it were shut off due to heavy snowfall. But don't worry, it wasn't all doom and gloom – they had a real hoot!

According to eyewitnesses (okay, it was just the guy running the shrine's snack bar), the overnight guests had a blast playing hide-and-seek among the Shinto shrines and shrubs, while being served an endless supply of hot chocolate and freshly baked snowflake-shaped cookies.

"It was like one big happy family," said Taro Yamada, the snack bar owner. "Except for the guy who tried to sneeze out a snowball – that didn't quite end well."

The visitors, ranging from toddlers to pensioners (okay, it was mostly just a bunch of weirdos), were initially planning on enjoying a quick pit stop at the shrine before heading home. However, with roads closed and visibility down to near zero, they found themselves stuck for the night.

"We thought we'd be out in no time," said Yumi Nakamura, one of the stranded visitors. "But then we realized that we had a fantastic excuse to have an impromptu slumber party – and who needs Netflix when you've got snow-covered mountain views?"

The shrine's staff, who were initially worried about providing for the unexpected guests, ended up having a blast too. They even set up a mini-game night with a "Guess Who's Afraid of Snow?" theme, which proved to be a real hit (pun intended).

"We didn't know what to expect," said Shrine Staffer Koji Tanaka. "But it turned out that we had the perfect storm for an unforgettable snow-day – and not just because of the actual snow!"

Witnesses claim she was heard yelling something about needing "more female toilets" as she skidded to a stop mere inches from a group of bemused schoolchildren.
