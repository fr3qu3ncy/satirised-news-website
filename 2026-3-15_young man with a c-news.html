WAR BETS GET WILD IN PREDICTION MARKETS

Prediction markets have become the go-to place for people to bet on just about anything, including the outcome of wars and nuclear detonation, sparking widespread criticism and calls for regulation.

"PREDICTION PANDEMONIUM: WAR BETS GET WILD IN PREDICTION MARKETS"

In a bizarre turn of events, prediction markets have become the go-to place for people to bet on just about anything - including the outcome of wars and the chances of nuclear detonation. Yes, you read that right. Wars.

It all started when Stew, a 35-year-old from Montana (and self-proclaimed "expert" in sports betting), placed a $10 bet on the odds of Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei being "out" by March 1st. We're not sure what that means, but we're pretty sure it's not good for Iran.

Meanwhile, Polymarket (because of course, there's an app for that) has hosted over $500m in bets related to the war in Iran - with users able to wager on everything from nuclear detonation to when US forces will enter Iran. Yes, because that's exactly what we want to encourage: betting on war.

And if you thought it couldn't get any weirder, Kalshi (another prediction market app) even had a billboard promoting their New York City mayoral election odds - right next to a giant photo of Donald Trump giving a thumbs up.

But wait, there's more! Critics are calling for a crackdown on these apps, citing concerns about war profiteering, national security risks, and insider trading. Who knew betting on wars could be so... lucrative?

Even the Biden administration got in on the action (pun intended), proposing to ban sports and politics-related event contracts - but then dropped it like a hot potato after Trump's election.

So, what's next? Will we see prediction markets offering odds on which country will invade another first? Only time will tell. But one thing is certain: these apps have opened up Pandora's box of weirdness and it's only going to get weirder from here.

"PREDICTION MARKET FIRM DEFENDS WAR BETS, CALLS THEM 'LEGITIMATE ECONOMIC FUNCTIONS'"

In a shocking turn of events, prediction market firm Polymarket has defended its war bets - calling them "legitimate economic functions". Yes, because nothing says "legitimate economic function" like betting on nuclear detonation.

The company's CEO (or whatever the equivalent is in the world of prediction markets) argued that users can hedge against risks triggered by events. We're not sure what that means either, but we're pretty sure it involves a lot of complicated math and/or profiteering.

Meanwhile, critics are calling foul - citing concerns about war profiteering, national security risks, and insider trading. Who knew predicting wars could be so... profitable?

"REPUBLICANS JOIN CALLS FOR CRACKDOWN ON PREDICTION MARKETS"

In a shocking turn of events, Republicans have joined the calls for a crackdown on prediction markets - citing concerns about war profiteering, national security risks, and insider trading.

It's unclear what triggered this sudden bout of sanity in the GOP, but we're not complaining. After all, someone has to keep these apps in check.

It's unclear what kind of damage has already been done, but we're pretty sure it involves a lot of complicated math and/or profiteering. Again.
