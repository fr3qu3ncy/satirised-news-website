BREAKING: Polar Bear Mother Adopting a Cub Like It's 1999

In a shocking turn of events, a polar bear mother in northern Canada has been spotted adopting a cub that is not biologically her own. Because who needs DNA tests when you've got maternal instincts, am I right? This rare occurrence has left researchers scratching their heads and wondering if they should get the mom a participation trophy.

According to sources, the five-year-old polar bear mother was first captured as she emerged from her maternity den this spring with one cub in tow. But then, out of nowhere, another cub appeared! The researchers are still trying to figure out what happened to the biological mom, but let's be real, she's probably off doing her own thing, sipping cosmopolitans in a trendy cafe.

This is only the 13th known case of adoption among the 4,600 bears studied for nearly five decades.
