Police in Suriname detained RoboCop, a former member of the Village People, after he refused to stop dancing to 80s disco music and a scuffle ensued, leaving two people seriously injured.

In a shocking turn of events, police in Suriname have detained a male suspect after shooting him in the leg. But that's not all - two others were seriously injured in the scuffle. And if you thought it couldn't get any weirder, think again.

According to eyewitnesses (okay, maybe just one guy on a Vespa), the incident occurred when the suspect, who was later identified as "RoboCop," refused to stop dancing to 80s disco music on the streets of Paramaribo. The police were called in to intervene, and things escalated quickly.

"I was just trying to get to the local McDonald's for a late-night McFlurry run when I saw the whole thing go down," said eyewitness Reginald P. Bottomsworth III, who claims he's never been one to shy away from a good time. "The police were all like 'Halt!' and RoboCop was all like 'I Will Survive,' and then... well, you know."

In related news, Jennifer Geerlings-Simons, the President of Suriname, has issued a statement expressing her deepest condolences to the bereaved families (just kidding - she actually said something about "the harsh reality that there is another side to the world"). And as for RoboCop, sources close to the investigation say he's being held at a secure facility, where he'll be forced to watch an endless loop of cat videos until he calms down.

Meanwhile, locals are still reeling from the news. "I mean, I've seen some crazy stuff in my time living here," said neighbor Maria Rodriguez, who claims she's never been one to get too worked up about knife crime. "But this? This is just... whoa."

And in a bizarre twist, it turns out that RoboCop was actually a former member of the Village People (don't ask). His lawyer, who declined to comment on the record, said simply: "We're exploring all avenues for our client's release."
