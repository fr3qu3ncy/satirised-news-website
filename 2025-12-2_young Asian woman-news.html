"5 Fingers, No People! The Bizarre Sock Epidemic That's Sweeping Indonesia!"

A cyclone in Indonesia has resulted in 500 people going missing, leaving behind only their socks, and the subsequent rescue efforts have been hindered by a lack of big vehicles, bureaucratic red tape, and environmental mismanagement.

BREAKING: Cyclone Chaos in Indonesia

In a bizarre turn of events, 500 people have gone missing after a cyclone hit the region, leaving behind only their socks and a trail of destruction. The incident occurred when a rare cyclone formed over the Malacca Strait, which is essentially just a really long stretch of water with some seagulls flying around.

Eyewitnesses claim that the floods were "like a tsunami," but not in a good way. Like, they were actually pretty bad and stuff happened. Arini Amalia, a resident from Aceh's Pidie Jaya Regency, was quoted as saying, "This is the worst in my life... and also in my grandmother's life... she's like 97 years old or something."

Rescue workers have been trying to reach affected areas on foot and by motorcycle, because why not? It's not like they had bigger vehicles to use. Pictures from the region show bridges washed away, roads covered in mud and debris, and logs piled high – it's like a giant game of Jenga up in there.

One resident, Mariana, was overheard saying, "Watching the excavators... I keep thinking what condition will my 15-year-old son be in when they find him?" Yeah, because that's exactly what you want to think about while waiting for rescue – your missing teenager being found covered in mud and debris.

The Indonesian government has been criticized for their slow response to the disaster, mainly due to bureaucratic red tape... or something. Some activist groups have also claimed that environmental mismanagement (read: deforestation) made things worse. On the other hand, President Prabowo Subianto said they're doing everything they can to overcome difficulties, but you know what? That's just what he said.

Meanwhile, in related news, 1,200 people have died across South and Southeast Asia due to flooding and landslides. But hey, at least the seagulls are fine, right?
