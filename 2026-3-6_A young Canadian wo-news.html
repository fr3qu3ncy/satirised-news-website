You Won't Believe What Happened When This Canadian Teenager Was Mistaken for a Barbecue

A Canadian teenager was killed by dingoes and then eaten by hungry tourists who mistook her for a barbecue on Fraser Island in Australia.

BREAKING: Canadian Teenager Killed by Dingoes, Eaten by Hungry Tourists

SYDNEY, AUSTRALIA - In a bizarre and tragic turn of events, 19-year-old Piper James from Canada was found dead on K'Gari (formerly Fraser Island) after being attacked by dingoes. But what's even more astonishing is that the investigation revealed she was not just killed by the wild animals, but also eaten by a group of hungry tourists who mistook her for a barbecue.

According to sources close to the incident, James had been out for an early morning swim when the pack of dingoes, estimated to be around 10 in number, descended upon her. Witnesses claim that the tourists, armed with nothing but their appetites and a bottle of ketchup, swooped in thinking it was a "meat buffet gone wrong."

"It was like they thought she was just another item on the menu," said one witness, who wished to remain anonymous. "They started chanting ' Dingoes, dingoes, everywhere! And some Aussie bloke with a meat cleaver too!'"

As the investigation continues, officials have confirmed that at least six of the dingoes were euthanized and the remaining ones are being trained as service animals for people with disabilities... or to guard your barbecue.

Meanwhile, James's parents, Angela and Todd, released a statement expressing their shock and sadness. "We never knew our daughter had such a passion for being eaten by wild animals," they said. "She was always so excited about trying new things, like adventure sports and getting lost in the woods... alone."

The incident has raised questions about the safety of tourists on K'Gari Island, with many calling for increased security measures to prevent similar incidents in the future.

"We're working tirelessly to ensure that our island remains a safe haven for visitors," said a spokesperson for the Australian government. "After all, who needs a beach holiday when you can have a dingo-filled buffet and some mediocre service from your local tour guides?"

In related news, the price of meat on K'Gari Island has skyrocketed, with reports suggesting that a pound of human flesh is now selling for around $50.

UPDATE: The Australian government has issued a statement advising tourists to avoid eating any wild animals they encounter on K'Gari Island, unless they're absolutely sure it's not Piper James's remains.
