EXPLOSION AT US EMBASSY IN OSLO: TERRORISM NOT RULED OUT, POLICE SAY (BUT WE'RE SURE IT WAS JUST A DISPUTED DELIVERY OF PIZZA)

Oslo, Norway - In a shocking turn of events, an overnight explosion at the US embassy in Oslo may have been an act of terrorism. Just kidding, it was probably just a bunch of rowdy college kids trying to get their hands on some free pizza from Domino's.

According to eyewitnesses (okay, fine, it was actually just a guy named Bjorn who was walking by), the explosion occurred at around 1am on Sunday morning outside the public entrance to the building. "I was just walking home from the pub when I heard a loud bang," said Bjorn, still shaking his head in confusion. "At first, I thought it was just someone playing a prank with a whoopee cushion, but then I saw all the police cars and ambulances showing up."

Police and forensic experts were quickly deployed to the scene, where they found minor damage to the embassy building. "One of the hypotheses is that it is an act of terrorism," said Frode Larsen, head of the police joint investigation and intelligence unit, in a statement. "But let's be real, we're not completely locked into it because we're not entirely sure what happened either."

In fact, sources close to the investigation (okay, fine, it was just a guy named Erik who works at the embassy) have revealed that the blast may have been caused by a dispute over the delivery of pizza. "It turns out that someone ordered a large pepperoni with extra cheese, but when it arrived, they realized they wanted anchovies instead," said Erik, chuckling. "Next thing you know, there's an explosion and everyone's running around like headless chickens."

Meanwhile, US diplomats were in contact with Norwegian authorities to discuss the incident. A spokesperson for the State Department said they were "aware of an incident at the US Embassy in Oslo" and were investigating (or as we like to call it, trying to figure out who ate the last slice of pizza).

The embassy's chargé d'affaires Eric Meyer was unavailable for comment, but sources close to him say he's still trying to get his hands on that free Domino's pizza.

UPDATE: The police have now confirmed that they've found a suspicious package containing a half-eaten pizza and a note that reads "You'll never take me alive, coppers!" It's unclear at this time what the note actually meant or who wrote it, but one thing's for sure - it was definitely not terrorism.
