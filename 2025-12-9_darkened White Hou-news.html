But instead, it became a battleground for nations trying to avoid taking responsibility for their carbon footprint. Specifically, the report recommends that countries move away from coal, oil, and gas like they're old gym socks – but US officials were having none of it.

"I mean, come on guys," said Dr. David Broadstock, lead author of the report, "we can't just keep burning fossil fuels like there's no tomorrow (which, let's be honest, is what's going to happen if we don't stop). But hey, who needs the future when you've got short-term gains and subsidies for fossil fuel companies, right? Saudi Arabia and Russia were also part of this hijacking crew, because what's more American than sticking it to the environment?

They didn't agree with anything we said on climate change, biodiversity, fossil fuels, plastics, and subsidies. It was like they were trying to win an argument with a toddler – all tantrums and no substance."
